Project Abstract

This website focuses on major themes from the Book of Mormon including faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, baptism, receiving the gift of the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end. Each theme is explored through scripture, exegesis, and eisegesis in order to better understand the meaning of these principles and how they point us to Jesus Christ. The purpose of this study is not only to increase scriptural understanding, but also to help apply these teachings in meaningful ways to daily life. At the end of the website, there is also a guessing game to help practice the difference between exegesis and eisegesis.

Faith in Jesus Christ

Key Scriptures:
  • Alma 32:21
  • Ether 12:6
  • 2 Nephi 25:26

Description

Faith in Jesus Christ is the foundation of the gospel. It is more than belief; it is trust in Christ that leads a person to act. The Book of Mormon teaches that faith helps us grow spiritually, gives us strength in trials, and leads us closer to the Savior.

Exegesis

In Alma 32, Alma is teaching the poor Zoramites who had been cast out of their synagogues. He explains that faith begins like a seed and grows as it is nourished. In its original context, the passage teaches that faith develops through patience, experiment, and spiritual effort.

Eisegesis

I can apply this scripture to my own life by seeing that even small acts of faith, such as prayer, scripture study, and obedience, can grow into stronger faith if I remain consistent.

How It Points to Jesus Christ and Why It Matters

Faith points directly to Jesus Christ because He is the object of our faith and the source of salvation. Without Him, faith has no saving power. This principle matters because faith is the starting point of the gospel and leads to repentance, covenant making, and spiritual growth.

Repentance

Key Scriptures:
  • Alma 36:18–21
  • Helaman 5:10–11
  • Mosiah 26:29–30

Description

Repentance is the process of turning away from sin and turning back to God. The Book of Mormon teaches that repentance brings forgiveness, peace, and lasting change through the Atonement of Jesus Christ.

Exegesis

In Alma 36, Alma is recounting his conversion after remembering the words of his father and calling upon Jesus Christ. The passage shows that repentance is not only sorrow for sin, but a complete spiritual change that comes through Christ.

Eisegesis

I can apply this passage to my own life by remembering that no matter how far I may feel from God, I can always turn back to Him and receive forgiveness and peace through Jesus Christ.

How It Points to Jesus Christ and Why It Matters

Repentance points to Jesus Christ because He is the one who makes forgiveness possible through His Atonement. This principle matters because it gives hope, healing, and the opportunity to change and return to God.

Baptism

Key Scriptures:
  • 2 Nephi 31:5–7
  • Mosiah 18:8–10
  • 3 Nephi 11:33

Description

Baptism is the first covenant ordinance of the gospel. It shows a willingness to follow Jesus Christ, keep His commandments, and take His name upon us.

Exegesis

In 2 Nephi 31, Nephi teaches that Jesus Christ Himself was baptized to fulfill all righteousness. In context, this shows that baptism is a necessary step of obedience and a required part of following Christ.

Eisegesis

I can apply this to my own life by remembering that baptism is not just something I did once, but a covenant that should shape the way I live every day.

How It Points to Jesus Christ and Why It Matters

Baptism points to Jesus Christ because He set the perfect example by being baptized Himself. It matters because it is the gate that begins the covenant path and formally shows our commitment to follow Him.

Receiving the Gift of the Holy Ghost

Key Scriptures:
  • 2 Nephi 32:5
  • Moroni 10:5
  • 3 Nephi 27:20

Description

The gift of the Holy Ghost allows us to receive guidance, comfort, truth, and spiritual strength. The Book of Mormon teaches that the Holy Ghost reveals truth and helps disciples stay close to God.

Exegesis

In Moroni 10:5, Moroni invites readers to ask God if the Book of Mormon is true and promises that truth will be revealed by the power of the Holy Ghost. In context, the scripture emphasizes that the Holy Ghost is a revealer of divine truth.

Eisegesis

I can apply this passage by trusting spiritual impressions more and seeking the guidance of the Holy Ghost in personal decisions, questions, and challenges.

How It Points to Jesus Christ and Why It Matters

The Holy Ghost points to Jesus Christ because He testifies of Christ, teaches truth about Him, and leads people to follow Him. This matters because we need divine guidance and confirmation in order to stay on the covenant path.

Enduring to the End

Key Scriptures:
  • 2 Nephi 31:20
  • 3 Nephi 15:9
  • Moroni 6:4

Description

Enduring to the end means continuing in faith, obedience, repentance, and devotion to Christ throughout life. It is a principle of steady discipleship and long-term commitment.

Exegesis

In 2 Nephi 31:20, Nephi teaches that disciples must press forward with a steadfastness in Christ, having a perfect brightness of hope. In context, the scripture teaches that salvation is not only about beginning the gospel path, but faithfully continuing on it.

Eisegesis

I can apply this scripture by remembering that discipleship is not about one spiritual moment, but about being consistent over time, even when life becomes difficult or routine.

How It Points to Jesus Christ and Why It Matters

Enduring to the end points to Jesus Christ because He gives the strength, grace, and hope needed to remain faithful. It matters because true discipleship requires continuing with Christ throughout life, not just starting with Him.
